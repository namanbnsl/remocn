Text is most of what a demo video says, so the typography tier got the most attention. Eighteen entrances and swaps, each built on Remotion's `interpolate` and `spring` rather than a CSS keyframe library — they seek correctly, and frame 40 looks the same on every render.

<ChangelogPreview name="soft-blur-in" />

### New components

Entrances — [Soft Blur In](/docs/typography/soft-blur-in), [Per Character Rise](/docs/typography/per-character-rise), [Bottom Up Letters](/docs/typography/bottom-up-letters), [Top Down Letters](/docs/typography/top-down-letters), [Spring Scale In](/docs/typography/spring-scale-in), [Micro Scale Fade](/docs/typography/micro-scale-fade), [Mask Reveal Up](/docs/typography/mask-reveal-up), [Line By Line Slide](/docs/typography/line-by-line-slide), [Kinetic Center Build](/docs/typography/kinetic-center-build), and [Focus Blur Resolve](/docs/typography/focus-blur-resolve).

Exits and swaps — [Blur Out Up](/docs/typography/blur-out-up), [Scale Down Fade](/docs/typography/scale-down-fade), [Fade Through](/docs/typography/fade-through), [Per Word Crossfade](/docs/typography/per-word-crossfade), [Shared Axis Y](/docs/typography/shared-axis-y), [Shared Axis Z](/docs/typography/shared-axis-z), [Short Slide Down](/docs/typography/short-slide-down), and [Short Slide Right](/docs/typography/short-slide-right).

Numbers got their own treatment a few days earlier — [Number Wheel](/docs/typography/number-wheel) and [Rolling Number](/docs/typography/rolling-number) — along with [Backdrop](/docs/layout/backdrop), the wrapper that gives any component a surface to sit on.
