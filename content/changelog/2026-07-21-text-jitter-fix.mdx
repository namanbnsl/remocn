---
title: Text animations no longer jitter on the landing
date: 2026-07-21
---

A caption easing into place did not glide — it held still, clicked down a pixel, held still, clicked again. Give every word or character its own stagger and those clicks land on different frames, so a ripple ran through the line while the rule right next to it stayed perfectly smooth.

That contrast is what made it visible, and it is not a Remotion bug. A glyph is rasterized into a cache that can only be placed on a whole device pixel vertically. Once an eased tail slows below roughly half a pixel per frame, nothing moves for several frames — then the accumulated offset crosses a boundary and the glyph jumps a whole pixel at once. Rectangles and borders have no such cache, so they shade their edge pixel instead and glide.

The fix splits the progress driver in two. Position now lands on a short window while opacity, blur, and colour keep the full curve — the part removed is the part the eye never read as movement, only as clicking. Entrances look the same; the ripple is gone.

### Fixes

- Travel windows clipped on [Soft Blur In](/docs/typography/soft-blur-in), [Per Character Rise](/docs/typography/per-character-rise), [Line By Line Slide](/docs/typography/line-by-line-slide), [Mask Reveal Up](/docs/typography/mask-reveal-up), [Bottom Up Letters](/docs/typography/bottom-up-letters), [Top Down Letters](/docs/typography/top-down-letters), [Short Slide Right](/docs/typography/short-slide-right), [Short Slide Down](/docs/typography/short-slide-down), and [Kinetic Center Build](/docs/typography/kinetic-center-build).
- Ease-in exits had the mirrored problem, with the creep at the start instead of the end. [Per Word Crossfade](/docs/typography/per-word-crossfade), [Blur Out Up](/docs/typography/blur-out-up), [Mask Reveal Up](/docs/typography/mask-reveal-up), and [Line By Line Slide](/docs/typography/line-by-line-slide) now delay the travel rather than clip it.
- [Short Slide Down](/docs/typography/short-slide-down) and [Kinetic Center Build](/docs/typography/kinetic-center-build) also reflow their earlier words every time a new one lands. Those pushes are the dominant motion in both components and had the same eased tail, so they are clipped to the same window as the entrance.
- [Staggered Fade Up](/docs/typography/staggered-fade-up) was already clean and is untouched. It is linear, so it never enters the sub-pixel zone — the proof of the rule rather than an exception to it.
